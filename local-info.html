Getting here

UC Santa Barbara is in Goleta, about 10 miles west of downtown Santa Barbara. The closest airport is Santa Barbara Airport (SBA); Los Angeles International (LAX) and Hollywood Burbank (BUR) are common alternatives.

  • From SBA: short trip to campus by taxi or rideshare.
  • From LAX: the Santa Barbara Airbus express bus and the Amtrak Pacific Surfliner train both connect to Goleta or Santa Barbara. Rideshare and rental cars are also options.
  • From BUR: rideshare, rental car, or transfer to Amtrak.

Local transit

Santa Barbara MTD serves the UCSB campus. Line 24x is an express from downtown Santa Barbara, and Line 11 also stops near the UCen.

Around Santa Barbara

A short list of nearby spots worth visiting:

On campus

  • Storke Tower: the iconic 175-foot bell tower at the center of campus, the tallest building in Santa Barbara County.
  • UCSB Lagoon & Lagoon Trail: an easy walking loop right next to the venue, with views of the Pacific and the Channel Islands.
  • Campus Point Beach: a popular surfing and tidepool spot at the southern edge of campus.
  • AD&A Museum: UCSB’s on-campus museum of art, design, and architecture (free admission).

In Santa Barbara

  • Downtown & State Street: the city’s main pedestrian corridor for shopping, restaurants, and the Spanish Colonial Revival architecture of the Santa Barbara County Courthouse (free tower views).
  • The Funk Zone: a walkable neighborhood of wine-tasting rooms, breweries, galleries, and casual dining near the waterfront.
  • Stearns Wharf & the Waterfront: the historic pier with seafood restaurants and views of the Channel Islands; East Beach and the harbor are next door.
  • Old Mission Santa Barbara: the “Queen of the Missions,” with gardens and a small museum.
  • Beaches: Campus Point Beach (next to the venue), Goleta Beach, Butterfly Beach, and East Beach.

For dining, options range from quick bites in Isla Vista (a short walk from campus) to highlights downtown such as La Super-Rica Taqueria (classic Mexican), Brophy Bros. (seafood on the harbor), and the many restaurants in the Funk Zone and along State Street.
